Carl G. Jung

Psychiatrist & psychologist 1875 - 1961

About

Carl Gustav Jung was a Swiss psychiatrist and psychoanalyst who at the beginning of his career was a supporter of Freud and shared with him the interest on the unconscious. As a result of his differences with his collegue, he founded analytical psychology, where he developed severeal known concepts such as archetypal phenomena, the collective unconscious, extraverd and introverd personalities.

The unconscious can realize itself only with the help of consciousness and under its constant coantrol. At the same time consciousness must keep one eye on the unconscious and the other focused just as clearly on the potentialities of human existence and human relationships.

Carl G. Jung

Letters Vol. 1, Pages 239-240

Timeline

  • 1875

    Was born on July 26 in Kesswil, in the Swiss canton of Thurgau.

  • 1895

    Began to study medicine at the University of Basel.

  • 1900

    Moved to Zürich and began working at he Burghölzli psychiatric hospital.

  • 1905

    Was appointed as a permanent 'senior' doctor at the hospital and also became a lecturer Privardozent in the medical faculty of Zurich University.

  • 1907

    Met Freud for the first time in Vienna on 3 March. Jung recalled the discussion between himself and Freud as interminable, unceasing for thirteen hours. Six months later, start an intense correspondence and collaboration that lasted six years.

  • 1912

    Pusblished Psychology of the Unconscious, which made manifest the developing theoretical divergence between Jung and Freud.

  • 1913 - 1914

    Spoke at meetings of the Psycho-Medical Society in London.

  • 1915

    Jung commissioned a large red leather-bound book, and began to transcribe his notes, along with painting, working intermittently for sixteen years. The Liber Novus or the Red Book was published on 7 October 2009.

  • Late nineteen-teens

    Jung emerged from his period of isolation in the late nineteen-teens with the publication of several journal articles, followed in 1921 with Psychological Types. There followed a decade of active publication, interspersed with overseas travels.

  • 1961

    Wrote his last work, a contribution to Man and His Symbols.Died on 6 June 1961 at Küsnacht.
